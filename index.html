Kingdoms

Today, there are generally considered to be 7 kingdoms of life, organized into 2 superkingdoms:

Superkingdom: Prokaryota

Bacteria

Bacteria are single-celled microorganisms...

Archaea

Archea are also single-celled, but different...

Superkingdom: Eukaryota

Protozoa

Protozoa are single-celled eukaryotes sometimes called "one-celled animals."

Chromista

There are a bunch of really tiny organisms in this kingdom, including Toxoplasma gondii which is a brain parasite you can get from cats.

Plantae

Trees, flowers, algae, and moss! These guys give us much-needed oxygen.

Fungi

Yeasts, molds, and mushrooms. These are famous for breaking down dead organisms.

Animalia

You, me, dogs, cats. Most things you find at the zoo too.
